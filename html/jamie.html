Chief of science, Jamie

As a Data Scientist, my mission is simple: convince people I'm advancing the future of software, while secretly breaking everything in the name of “innovation.” I specialize in releasing beta versions that never leave beta, and publishing research papers no one — not even I — fully understand.

I design algorithms that promise to change the world, but in reality can't even sort a list without crashing. My machine learning models are trained on datasets so biased that even my coffee machine refuses to recognize me without a lab coat.

I offer “open-source contributions” that require 200 dependencies, three obscure compilers, and a ritual sacrifice just to run “Hello World.” Every bug I fix introduces two new ones — but that's not a mistake, that's job security.

If you installed my software, congratulations — your bugs are now my legacy. And if you cited my research, well… let's just say the replication crisis now has a new mascot.
